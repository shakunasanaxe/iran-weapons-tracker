Strategy & Trends

Iranian attack strategy compared to US/Israeli response — by day and by phase. Plus trends in Iranian launch degradation.

Iran's Attack Strategy

Phase 1: Shock (Feb 28–Mar 2)

Massive barrages — 182 ballistic missiles Day 1. Simultaneous strikes on Gulf bases, Israel, embassies. Goal: overwhelm air defenses.

Phase 2: Sustained (Mar 3–7)

Shift to drones + smaller missile volleys. Naval sabotage: 10 ships destroyed in Hormuz. Asymmetric attacks on embassies, airports.

Phase 3: Attrition (Mar 8–12)

~24 missiles + ~48 drones/day. Cluster munitions on Israel. Oil infrastructure targeting (Ruwais, Fujairah, BAPCO).

Phase 4: Degraded (Mar 13–17)

60%+ launchers neutralized. Continued drone attacks on oil/airports. 12 waves against Israel on Mar 16.

US & Israel Strategy

Phase 1: Decapitation (Feb 28–Mar 1)

~200 aircraft hitting 500–1,000 targets. B-2s from US soil. GBU-28 bunker busters on nuclear sites. Khamenei targeted killing.

Phase 2: Naval Supremacy (Mar 2–4)

19 ships + sub destroyed. IRIS Dena sunk by torpedo. Natanz nuclear site hit. 2,000 targets struck total.

Phase 3: Systematic Destruction (Mar 5–10)

Hundreds of missile sites struck. 3rd carrier deployed. 16 mine-laying vessels destroyed. Focus on defense industry.

Phase 4: Consolidation (Mar 11–17)

300+ ballistic sites rendered inoperable. 260–290 of 410–440 launchers destroyed. 18 bombers at RAF Fairford.
